About Pheha Plastic

Pheha Plastic is a new recycling (not-for-profit) company, established in partnership with Hirundo Lesotho. The bulk of what we do comes down to the use of three machines that transform plastic through a process of shredding and heating, thereby creating beautiful and useful products out of discarded plastic.

Mission

To create a greener Lesotho through plastic recycling

Sanitation
- Clean up the environment (creating more beautiful communities)
Recycling
- Divert plastic from becoming trash and polluting the environment
Awareness
- Engage community members in environmental action through awareness campaigns, clean-ups and work-shops
Waste Management
- Provide sustainable plastic waste management options for households and businesses.

Vision

In addition to being a regional recycling centre, we aim to provide an economic boost to the community of Morija and surrounding villages. Though we are starting small, our aim is to increase our production capacity to create employment for local people – both through the collection of plastic, and as part of our production staff. An essential part of our vision is the establishment of an on-going recycling outreach program (“Plastic Reach”) to be introduced in schools and community centers around the country. The aim of these sessions will be to raise awareness for environmentalism and also to encourage every-day climate activism that centers on taking action to improve our communities. By adding value to plastic, we aim to change the way it is regarded in our society so that it is collected instead of disposed of. Plastic can then be delivered to our premises in exchange for credit, tokens or vouchers.

Plastic REACH

The Plastic Reach project is targeting schools because of the existing set up of the school, in terms of space and children in one place, already students are taking turns in going to school and that eliminates traffic to be able to keep Covid-19 precautionary measures in mind. Our sessions can accommodate 60 students: this allows us to separate them into three groups of 20, who will be able to socially distance while carrying out the sessions (hence the three stations).
